### Post 1 — Emirates Pride Inventory Management

Built something I'm genuinely proud of: a full inventory and sales management system for Emirates Pride Perfumes, deployed across 35+ outlets in UAE and Oman.

The challenge: a luxury fragrance brand operating at scale, with no unified system for stock tracking, transfers, or management reporting. Daily decisions were being made on guesswork.

What we built:
- iPad-optimised daily stock grid for outlet staff
- GRN (Goods Received Notes) and inter-outlet transfer workflows
- Bilingual EN/AR interface — because in the UAE, one language is never enough

Stack: HTML/JS frontend + Supabase (PostgreSQL) backend. Designed to work reliably in retail environments without heavy infrastructure.

### Post 2 — AI Supply Chain Platform

What if your supply chain could flag a chargeback risk before the invoice even landed?

That's the core idea behind the AI Supply Chain Platform I've been building — two prototypes targeting real pain points in retail supply chains:

**Compliance Shield (OTIF Copilot)**
Retail suppliers to Walmart, Target, and Noon UAE face brutal OTIF penalties. Compliance Shield uses the Claude API + pgvector RAG to detect OTIF failures before chargebacks hit, and auto-drafts dispute letters using your own historical data. Early detection. Real savings.

**Neural Command (Exception Brain)**
Ingests 2,000–5,000 supply chain events per day, scores and triages them with AI, and generates daily S&OP briefs so ops teams focus on what matters — not noise.

Built for 3PL ops teams drowning in alerts with no intelligent layer to prioritise them.

Live demo: vinayak682.github.io/ai-supply-chain-platform/

This is the kind of AI application that moves the needle — not a chatbot, but an operational intelligence layer.

### Post 3 — SupplyMind SaaS

I've spent the last two years as a Demand Planning Analyst watching the same problems repeat: misaligned S&OP cycles, reactive exception management, and planning tools that require a data science team to operate.

So I'm building SupplyMind — a SaaS platform purpose-built for the UAE/GCC supply chain market.

Two modules in development:

**S&OP Copilot** — Guides planners through the S&OP cycle with AI-assisted consensus building, scenario modelling, and meeting prep. Less spreadsheet wrestling, more strategic decision-making.

**Event Surge Planner** — Anticipates demand spikes from regional events (Ramadan, DSF, National Day) and translates them into actionable inventory and procurement signals.

Stack: Next.js 16 + Supabase + Claude API.

The GCC market has unique demand rhythms that global platforms don't account for. That's the gap SupplyMind addresses.

Still in build — but if you're a supply chain leader in the region curious about early access or collaboration, my DMs are open.
